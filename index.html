Innovators didn't invent products.
They invented systems for other people to use.

Tesla didn't power your house. He built the grid that did. The Wright Brothers didn't invent a flying machine — they invented the discipline of piloting it. Edison didn't make a lightbulb so you could read at night. He built the factory, the patent, and the utility company that delivered light to a continent.

The leverage was never the gadget. It was the architecture beneath it.

Most of us were taught to work by people who didn't build anything that lasted. The advice we got was for surviving systems, not building them. So we end up busy without becoming useful.

This is a series — and a studio — for people trying to do it the other way around. Build the system. Make the leverage. Work different.
